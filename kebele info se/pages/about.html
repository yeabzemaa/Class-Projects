Kebele Administration

Kebele is the smallest administrative unit in the federal structure of Ethiopia. A kebele is a neighborhood or a localized rural community in a woreda (district) that has a governing council and a local administrative structure.

The kebele system was established in the 1970s after the overthrow of Emperor Haile Selassie's monarchy. It was part of the administrative reforms introduced by the Derg military junta that came to power. The Derg aimed to decentralize governance and bring it closer to the local population.

Each kebele has an elected council and a chairperson. The kebele council is responsible for local administration, development projects, security, and maintaining public services within its jurisdiction. Kebele officials also play a role in distributing land and other government resources to residents.

Kebeles vary greatly in size, but on average they have a population of 5,000 to 10,000 people in urban areas and up to 30,000 in rural areas. There are over 15,000 kebeles across Ethiopia's regional states and city administrations.
